Problem 2

6-bit Binary → Decimal

Binary Unsigned Signed
000101 5 5
101011 43 –21
111111 63 –1
100000 32 –32

Formulas

Unsigned:
\( \text{value} = b_5 \cdot 2^5 + b_4 \cdot 2^4 + b_3 \cdot 2^3 + b_2 \cdot 2^2 + b_1 \cdot 2^1 + b_0 \cdot 2^0 \)

Signed (two’s complement):
\( \text{value} = -b_5 \cdot 2^5 + b_4 \cdot 2^4 + b_3 \cdot 2^3 + b_2 \cdot 2^2 + b_1 \cdot 2^1 + b_0 \cdot 2^0 \)

General case
$$ \text{value} = -b_5 \cdot 2^5 + \sum_{i=0}^{4} b_i \cdot 2^i $$

Problem 8

4-bit Binary Addition

1. Unsigned Numbers (7, 9)

Convert to 4-bit Binary

  • 7 → 0111
  • 9 → 1001

Binary Addition
0111 + 1001 = 10000

Result

  • 4-bit result: 0000
  • Carry out: 1
  • Decimal result: 16 (overflow in 4-bit unsigned representation)

2. Signed Numbers (4, -5)
(Using 4-bit Two’s Complement)

Convert to 4-bit Binary

  • 4 → 0100

Convert -5 to Two’s Complement

  1. +5 → 0101
  2. Invert bits → 1010
  3. Add 1 → 1011

-5 → 1011

Binary Addition
0100 + 1011 = 1111

Result

  • Binary: 1111
  • Decimal (Two’s Complement): -1
